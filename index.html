I am against Apple's monopoly.

This issue goes beyond gaming.

I’m a hobbyist developer who tries to push the limits of Apple’s walled garden. I want to create experiences that are both quality and are unique. This includes emulators, free applications, and tools to enrich the experience of iOS. And Apple doesn’t like this.

So I fought back. I found ways to allow myself and others to use our devices as we want, and I continue to advocate for using Apple's devices our way, not Apple's.

Eclipse is an emulator marketed towards iOS users that I helped develop. It exists as a web app because Apple won’t allow emulators on the App Store and make it near-impossible to reasonably sideload on iOS.

On Android? Sideloading is a setting you can enable. On iOS? You have to "resign" them weekly and can only have up to three apps at a time, lest you use an exploit to "jailbreak" your device.

Speaking of, Sileo, Zebra, Installer, and Cydia all allow users to install tens of thousands of packages that Apple doesn’t permit onto their devices. However, a security vulnerability is required, meaning Apple is constantly fighting back (as they should). It may be for your safety to do so, but also means these stores are increasingly hard to use.

Some people found ways to install their own apps without a jailbreak. Tools like Zeus and AltStore use their own techniques to circumvent the App Store, but both are crippled by, and frowned upon, by Apple. Zeus requires the abuse of enterprise certificates and AltStore has a cap of the amount of apps you can install. If you want to install more, you have to cough up $100 a year to be considered a “developer.”

This isn’t fair play. This is Apple abusing their power for profit. And I can’t stand for that.

This isn’t a new fight, either It’s been happening for years, often being fought in the background in small communities of developers and user. Despite Epic Games suing Apple to maximize their profit and launch a competing store on iOS, allowing users to sideload applications without handicap is important for user freedom.

It's about time something is done. For real.

Educate yourself.
